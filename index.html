The book

Hi Guys, I have the pleasure to present to you one of my favorite books of all time.

This book is very dear to my heart for so many reasons, one being that it was written by my favorite author Mrs. Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie.

Ladies and gentlemen, today I'm going to tell you more about Purple Hibiscus.

Dear passengers, please fasten your seatbelt. We're about to take off. Our next destination: Nigeria.

The Story

Imagine growing up in a house where your dad is basically a dictator, but instead of a throne, he rules with Bible verses and terrifying punishments. That’s Kambili’s life in Purple Hibiscus.

She’s a shy, thoughtful fifteen-year-old living under her father Eugene’s strict rules. He’s a local hero on the outside, generous, respected, super religious, but at home, he’s scary and controlling.

Everything changes when Kambili and her brother Jaja visit their aunt Ifeoma, who’s basically the cool opposite of their dad.

Her house is full of laughter, independence, and chaos (the good kind), and she even grows these magical purple hibiscus flowers, symbols of freedom and courage.

For the first time, Kambili sees what life could be like outside her father’s shadow.

The story is about finding your voice, surviving abuse, and daring to grow your own “purple hibiscus” even when life is harsh. It’s dramatic, heartbreaking, sometimes funny, and ultimately a story about hope, rebellion, and the messy, beautiful ways families shape us.

Why read the book?

For those who dare to ask the following question: "Why Should we read this book?", here are 5 good reasons:

  1. Unforgettable characters: From tyrannical Eugene to warm, free-spirited Aunt Ifeoma, messy, real, and full of life.

  2. Big themes: Domestic abuse, religious hypocrisy, personal freedom, and family loyalty, all handled with honesty and depth.

  3. Experience Nigeria: From bustling streets to colorful gardens, the food, traditions, and everyday life jump off the page.

  4. Beautiful, vivid writing: Adichie’s prose is lyrical, descriptive, and full of sensory detail, you can almost hear, smell, and feel it.

  5. Think and reflect: Challenges ideas of authority, bravery, and the courage it takes to grow and stand up for yourself.

Bottom line, it’s a story of fear, freedom, resilience, and hope, a book that sticks with you long after you turn the last page.

I hope you're convinced now :).

The Author

Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie is a celebrated Nigerian author, storyteller, and public speaker known for her vivid, thought-provoking writing.

Her novels, including Purple Hibiscus, Half of a Yellow Sun, Americanah and Dream count, explore themes of identity, culture, love, and personal freedom while reflecting on Nigeria’s history and contemporary society.

She blends deeply personal stories with broader social and political insights, creating characters and worlds that feel both authentic and universal.

Beyond fiction, Adichie is a prominent voice in feminism and global literature, using her words to challenge assumptions, inspire empathy, and help readers see the world in new and powerful ways.
